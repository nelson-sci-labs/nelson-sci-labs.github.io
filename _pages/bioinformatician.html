Job Opening

Bioinformatician

Nelson Scientific Labs seeks a bioinformatician/data scientist with a strong background in biological sciences to support big data analyses, prediction model-building, and custom software development capabilities for our federally funded projects. The successful candidate holds a Master's degree in bioinformatics, biostatistics, computational biology, computer science, or closely related discipline. The successful candidate also brings a sincere desire to learn, adapt to new challenges, and develop novel approaches to harmonizing multiple large datasets used in medical device research and development. This position requires the incumbent have the ability to work independently or as part of a multi- disciplinary team as well as a solid programming and analytical skill set, which includes competencies in R, Python, and database applications. Experience with metabolomics, lipidomics, proteomics, and/or transcriptomics is highly desirable.


Responsibilities include but are not limited to the following:
  • Conduct and report on advanced and innovative multi-omics data analyses to support biomarker discovery, confirmation, and validation;
  • Use of pathway databases such as METLIN Gen2 and KEGG pathway and compound to test hypotheses, identify features of interest, and build molecular mechanisms underlying phenotypes under investigation;
  • Implement machine learning and AI tools for the analysis and integration of multi-omics data including transcriptomics, proteomics and miRNA data sets;
  • Develop and maintain an integrated, R/Python-based analytical pipeline based around instrument manufacturers' software and/or from publicly available resources, and/or de novo, for processing raw data (peak alignment, background subtraction, feature extraction, selection of statistically significant features);
  • Develop software tool for risk assessment using biomarker and clinical outcome data
Required Qualifications & Experience:
  • MS from an accredited program in bioinformatics, biostatistics, or another closely related field.
  • Minimum 2 years relevant work experience
  • Experience with analyzing multi-dimensional omics data
  • Solid programming skills (e.g. Java, C++, perl)
  • Experience with R/BioConductor
  • Demonstrated track-record of supporting data science research
  • Strong written and oral communication skills
Preferred Qualifications & Experience:
  • PhD in bioinformatics, biostatistics, or computational biology
  • Grant- and/or contract-writing participation at the state and federal levels
  • Experience in building and maintaining databases and statistical analysis plans and workflows
  • Familiarity with FDA in vitro diagnostic device software (i.e. software as device) regulatory guidelines
Working at Nelson:

The long-term mission of Nelson Scientific Labs. LLC is to leverage state of the art molecular phenotyping technologies for biomarker development to facilitate personalized medicine approaches. The new NIH award focuses on multi-omics- based biomarker device development for prediction of radiation induced heart dysfunction.
